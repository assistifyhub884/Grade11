1
Measurement
Scope of physics, SI, errors, precision/accuracy, significant figures, dimensions

1.1 Scope of Physics

  • Describe the importance of physics in science, technology and society. Physics explains how nature works and underpins engineering, medicine, electronics, and climate science — from MRI scanners to satellites and power generation.

1.2 S.I. Units

  • Describe SI base, derived and supplementary units. Base units (m, kg, s, A, K, mol, cd) combine to form derived units (N, J, Pa…).
  • Show derived units as products/quotients of base units. e.g., 1 N = 1 kg·m·s−2; 1 Pa = 1 N·m−2.

1.3 Errors & Uncertainty

  • Differentiate systematic vs. random errors. Systematic shift results from bias (e.g., zero error), random scatter from unpredictable fluctuations.
  • Measure uncertainty in a derived quantity. Propagate percentage/absolute uncertainties through calculations (sum, product, powers).

1.4 Precision & Accuracy

  • State differences between precision and accuracy. Precision = repeatability (spread), accuracy = closeness to true value; both can differ.

1.5 Significant Figures

  • Calculate with correct scientific notation and sig. figures. Round to the least precise data; keep track of decimal places in +/− and of significant digits in ×/÷.
  • Least count of an instrument. Smallest readable increment (e.g., vernier caliper LC = 0.01 cm).

1.6 Dimensions

  • Test homogeneity of equations with dimensions. Both sides must reduce to the same base dimensions (M, L, T, …).
  • Derive formulas using dimensional analysis. Infer forms like T ∝ √(L/g) for a pendulum by balancing dimensions.
2
Vectors & Equilibrium
Coordinate systems, vector addition, products, torque, conditions of equilibrium

2.1 Cartesian Coordinate System

  • Describe 2D/3D Cartesian systems. Use (x,y) or (x,y,z) axes to specify position and resolve components.

2.2 Head‑to‑Tail Rule

  • Explain vector sum by head‑to‑tail. Place vectors sequentially; the resultant joins start to finish.
  • Define resultant, negative, unit, null, position, equal vectors. Resultant = sum; negative = opposite direction; unit = magnitude 1; null = 0; position = from origin to a point; equal = same magnitude & direction.
  • Resolve a vector into rectangular components. V → (Vx, Vy, Vz) using trig.

2.3 Perpendicular Components

  • Determine sums via components. Add x- and y- components separately; recombine for magnitude/angle.

2.4 Scalar (Dot) Product

  • Define dot product. A·B = AB cosθ, a scalar measuring alignment.
  • Describe angle dependence & properties. Max at θ=0°, zero at 90°; distributive, commutative.

2.5 Vector (Cross) Product

  • Define cross product. A×B = AB sinθ n̂, a vector perpendicular to the plane; right‑hand rule.
  • Describe angle dependence & properties. Zero at θ=0°/180°; anti‑commutative; follows distributive law.

2.6 Torque

  • Torque as r×F. Tends to rotate; magnitude rF sinθ; lever arm concept.
  • Applications. Spanners, door handles, steering wheels, turbines.

2.7 Equilibrium of Forces

  • Define equilibrium & types. Translational (∑F=0) and rotational (∑τ=0); stable/unstable/neutral.
  • First & second conditions with examples. A balanced see‑saw or wall ladder shows ∑F=0 and ∑τ=0 simultaneously.
3
Motion & Force
Kinematics, Newton’s laws, momentum, collisions, projectiles

3.1 Displacement

  • Define displacement. Vector change in position; differs from distance.

3.2 Velocity & Acceleration

  • Average vs. instantaneous velocity. Δx/Δt vs. derivative dx/dt.
  • Accelerations. Average Δv/Δt; instantaneous dv/dt.
  • v‑t graphs & area. Slope = acceleration; area = displacement for straight‑line motion.

3.3 Equations of Motion

  • Uniform acceleration relations. v=u+at, s=ut+½at², v²=u²+2as; extend to free‑fall (g) without air resistance.

3.4 Newton’s Laws

  • State three laws. Inertia; F=ma; action–reaction.

3.5 Force, Momentum & Impulse

  • F and rate of change of momentum. F = dp/dt; ties dynamics to momentum.
  • Impulse. Product of force and interaction time; equals Δp.
  • Conservation of momentum. Closed system total p constant.
  • Elastic head‑on collisions (1D). Momentum and kinetic energy conserved; use simultaneous equations.
  • Force from water flow. Jet/flow changes momentum → thrust/force on surfaces.
  • Explosions. Internal forces separate parts with equal and opposite momenta; center of mass continues uniformly.
  • Rocket propulsion. Ejecting mass backward gives forward thrust; T ≈ ṁ ve − mg − drag.

3.6 Projectile Motion

  • Define projectile & trajectory. Curved path under uniform g with no air resistance.
  • Describe 2D motion. Independent horizontal (constant v) and vertical (uniform g) components.
  • Time, height, range. T=2u sinθ/g; H=u² sin²θ/(2g); R=u² sin2θ/g; use in numericals.
  • Ballistic missiles. Idealized arcs follow projectile formulas; guidance/drag alter real paths.
4
Work, Power & Energy
Work with angles, power, energies, conservation, escape velocity

4.1 Work

  • Define work. W = F·s = Fs cosθ; energy transfer by a force over a displacement.
  • Force at an angle. Only the component along displacement does work.
  • Units. Joule (J) = N·m; non‑SI like erg may appear historically.
  • Positive/negative/zero work. Helping, opposing, or perpendicular to motion.
  • Variable vs. constant force. Use area under F–s curve or integrate W=∫F·ds.

4.2 Work in a Gravitational Field

  • Explain work by gravity. Path‑independent; relates to changes in gravitational potential energy.

4.3 Power

  • Define power & dimension. Rate of doing work; [M L² T⁻³].
  • Units. Watt (W) = J·s⁻¹.
  • P = F·v. For motion at velocity v under force F; useful in vehicles and lifts.

4.4 Energy

  • Define energy. Capacity to do work.
  • Potential vs. kinetic. Stored by position/configuration vs. motion (½mv²).
  • Units. Joule; kWh for electrical energy.

4.5 Work–Energy

  • Relate work and energy with/without friction. Net work equals change in kinetic energy; include Wfriction=−f s in resistive media.

4.6 Absolute Gravitational Energy

  • Gravitational PE from infinity. U(r)=−GMm/r; zero at infinity.
  • Derivation. Integrate gravitational force radially.

4.7 Escape Velocity

  • Concept & formula. Minimum speed to reach infinity: ve=√(2GM/R); compute for Earth/Moon.

4.8 Conservation of Energy

  • State law & demonstrate in resistive media. Total energy converts form‑to‑form; mechanical energy decreases when work dissipates as heat/sound.

4.9 Energy Sources

  • Conventional & non‑conventional; uses. Fossil fuels vs. solar, wind, hydro, nuclear; evaluate efficiency and impact.
5
Circular Motion
Angular kinematics, centripetal acceleration, rotation, satellites

5.1 Angular Motion

  • Define θ, ω, α & link with linear motion. s=rθ, v=rω, at=rα.

5.2 Centripetal Quantities

  • Define ac & Fc. ac=v²/r=ω²r; inward force required for circular motion.

5.3 Moment of Inertia

  • Define I. Resistance to angular acceleration; depends on mass distribution.

5.4 Angular Momentum

  • Define L, units, conservation. L=Iω; conserved without external torque (e.g., skater pull‑in).

5.5 Rotational KE

  • KErot = ½Iω² and numericals. Add to translational for rolling bodies.

5.6 Satellites & Weightlessness

  • Weightlessness & types of satellites. Apparent weight zero in free‑fall; LEO/MEO/GEO; geostationary conditions and altitude.
  • Artificial gravity. Rotation supplies centripetal force mimicking weight.

5.7 Orbital Velocity

  • vorb derivation. v=√(GM/r); apply in calculations.
6
Fluid Dynamics
Laminar/turbulent, continuity, Bernoulli, viscosity, terminal velocity

6.1 Flow Types

  • Streamline vs. turbulent; conditions. Laminar has ordered layers; turbulence above critical Reynolds number.

6.2 Continuity Equation

  • Derive A v = constant (incompressible). Mass conservation; rocket mass flow links to thrust.
  • Apply to problems. Calculate speeds/areas.

6.3 Bernoulli’s Equation

  • Derivation & applications. Pressure + kinetic + potential head constant along streamline; aerofoil lift, venturi meters, atomizers.
  • Problem solving. Use pressure–speed–height trade‑offs.

6.4 Viscous Fluids

  • Viscous vs. non‑viscous; drag. Viscosity creates resistive force opposing motion through fluids.

6.5 Fluid Friction & Stokes’ Law

  • Define fluid friction; confirm Stokes’ form via dimensions. Fd ∝ η r v for small spheres at low Re.
  • Terminal velocity derivation. Balance weight, upthrust, and viscous drag.

6.6 Terminal Velocity

  • Define & dependencies. Depends on size, density difference, viscosity.
7
Oscillations
SHM, phase, springs, pendulum, energy, damping/resonance

7.1 SHM Basics

  • Key terms. Oscillatory/periodic motion; T, f, A; Hooke’s law F=−kx (state & derive); acceleration a=−ω²x.

7.2 Circular Motion Link

  • Project SHM from uniform circular motion. x=A cos(ωt+φ); derive v and a.

7.3 Phase

  • Define phase angle & displacement expression. Sets the initial state of oscillation.

7.4 Mass–Spring (Horizontal)

  • Instantaneous velocity. v=±ω√(A²−x²); relates to energy exchange.

7.5 Simple Pendulum

  • Pendulum does SHM for small angles. T=2π√(L/g); use in numericals.

7.6 Energy in SHM

  • PE, KE, total constant. Energy swaps between ½kx² and ½mv², sum = ½kA².

7.7 Free vs. Forced

  • Definitions & examples. Free oscillate at natural frequency; forced respond to driving frequency.

7.8 Resonance

  • Phenomenon & examples. Large amplitude at fdrive=f0; bridges, musical instruments.

7.9 Damped Oscillations

  • Define & applications. Energy lost to resistive forces; shock absorbers, seismographs.
8
Waves
Wave motion, speed of sound, interference, beats, stationary waves, Doppler

8.1 Wave Motion

  • Define & classify waves. Progressive, periodic; transverse vs. longitudinal; v=fλ (use in numericals); waves transfer energy, not matter.

8.2 Speed of Sound

  • Medium dependence & formulas. Newton’s formula with Laplace’s correction; effects of pressure, density, temperature; empirical v≈v₀+0.61t (°C).

8.3 Superposition

  • Principle & effects. Interference patterns; beats when close frequencies combine.

8.4 Stationary Waves

  • Formation & features. Nodes/antinodes; strings and air columns; modes follow L=nλ/2 for strings.
